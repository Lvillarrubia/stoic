
The 10 Daily Practices

1. Wake up early: Start the day deliberately, not passively. Decide how you will think, act, and carry yourself.

2. Train the body: Exercise daily. Build strength, endurance, and discipline. A careless body often weakens the mind.

3. Practice self-command: Before the day begins, remember: control your thoughts, your words, your impulses, your attention.

4. Journal and examine the mind: Write clearly. Set intentions, confront weaknesses, correct confusion, and keep your vision sharp.

5. Read and learn with purpose: Study what strengthens judgment, character, and understanding. Read with purpose, not just for stimulation.

6. Walk and reflect: Walk without noise when possible. Use that time to think, observe, and return to clarity.

7. Make time for deep work: Protect time for real work. Focus fully. A man cannot be careless with his attention or his craft.

8. Visualize and prepare for difficulty: Picture the day ahead. Expect difficulty, delays, conflict, temptation, and distraction. Rehearse your response in advance.

9. Life is short; act with urgency: Memento mori. Not for drama, but for proportion. Life is short, so act with urgency, dignity, and precision.

10. Review the day at night: Ask: What did I do well? Where was I weak? Where did I lose command? What will I correct tomorrow?
